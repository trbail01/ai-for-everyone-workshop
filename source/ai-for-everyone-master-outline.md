# AI for Everyone – Lesson Plan & Workshop Outline

**Length:** 2 Hours  
**Audience:** General public (non-technical background)  
**Format:** Interactive presentation + live demos + group activities  

---

## Purpose
This workshop introduces the general public to Artificial Intelligence in a friendly, accessible way. Participants will learn what AI is (and isn’t), explore real-world examples, practice writing better prompts, and understand how to use AI tools safely and responsibly.

---

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this session, participants will be able to:  
- Explain what AI is and where it appears in daily life.  
- Identify three opportunities and three limitations of AI.  
- Apply safe and responsible practices when using AI tools.  
- Create effective prompts that lead to better results.  

---

## Goals
- Demystify AI and show its everyday uses.  
- Teach safe, ethical, and responsible AI use.  
- Encourage critical thinking and fact-checking.  
- Build confidence in using AI tools effectively.  
- Provide practical, free resources participants can explore.  

---

## Materials Needed
- Slides or projector  
- Handouts:  
  - *Top 5 AI Tips*  
  - *Prompting Cheat Sheet*  
  - *Free Tools List*  
- Laptop with internet access for live demos  
- Whiteboard or flip chart for group brainstorms  
- Optional: participant laptops or phones for hands-on prompts  

---

## Workshop Flow & Activities

### 1. Welcome & Icebreaker (10 minutes)
- Introduce yourself and explain the purpose of the workshop.  
- **Poll/Discussion:**  
  - Who has used ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, or another AI tool?  
  - [What’s one word that comes to mind when you hear “AI”?](https://www.menti.com/al6q875mxign)
- Outline the session agenda and goals. 

---

### 2. What is AI? Everyday Examples (20 minutes)
- Explain AI simply: *pattern recognition + prediction from data.*  
- Everyday examples: spam filters, Netflix recommendations, Google Maps, Siri/Alexa.  
- **Activity – “AI Around Us”**  
  - In pairs/groups, list 3 ways you already use AI.  
  - Share one idea with the full group.  

---

### 3. What’s Possible (and Not) (25 minutes)
- **Demo:** Use an AI tool to write a poem, recipe, or quick summary.  
- Show an example where the AI makes an error (factually wrong or silly).  
- **Activity – “Would You Trust AI To…?”**  
  - Drive a car?  
  - Write legal advice?  
  - Recommend health tips?  
  - Plan your vacation?  
- Discuss: what tasks feel “safe” for AI, and which still need human judgment?  

---

### 4. Break (5 minutes)

---

### 5. Safe & Responsible AI Use (30 minutes)
- **Key Safety Points:**  
  - Never share personal or sensitive information.  
  - Always double-check facts (AI can be confidently wrong).  
  - Be cautious of scams, fake images, or deepfakes.  
  - Respect copyright and ethical concerns.  
- **Activity – “Spot the Fake”**  
  - Show 2–3 examples (AI-generated vs. real text or photos).  
  - Have participants guess which is which and discuss.  
- Tie-in with **Attorney General** training themes: scams, phishing, and identity theft.  

---

### 6. Prompting 101 (20 minutes)
- Explain what a “prompt” is and why specificity matters.  
- Show poor vs. improved prompt examples:  
  - *Poor:* “Write an email.”  
  - *Improved:* “Write a polite 3-paragraph email to my landlord about fixing a leaking sink.”  
- **Activity – “Prompt Challenge”**  
  - Participants write a prompt (e.g., “Write a bedtime story about a cat and a tractor”).  
  - Try a few live and discuss why some worked better.  

---

### 7. Wrap-Up & Resources (10 minutes)
- Recap:  
  - What AI is (and isn’t)  
  - How to use AI safely and effectively  
  - Why clear prompts matter  
- Distribute *Top 5 AI Tips* and *Free Tools* handouts.  
- Final Q&A and participant reflection:  
  - One thing learned  
  - One way they plan to use AI  
- Closing thought: *“AI is a tool — use it wisely, and it can be powerful.”*

- **Informal:** Observe engagement during group discussions and demos.  
- **End-of-session:** Quick 1-minute written reflection:  
  - *One thing you learned today*  
  - *One way you plan to try AI responsibly*

## Key Takeaways
- AI is already part of daily life.  
- Be curious — but stay cautious.  
- Use AI as a helper, not a replacement for judgment.  
- Clear, specific prompts produce better results.  
- Always verify and think critically about AI output.

- **Canva** – AI tools for text and design

---

## Instructor Notes
- Encourage discussion and curiosity.  
- Keep examples lighthearted (avoid politics, religion, or sensitive topics).  
- If participants are hesitant, show relatable examples (email drafting, recipes, summaries).  
- For live demos, use simple, visual tasks that show both strengths and limitations.
